Right now, Business Review Targets for Mid-market customers are calculated by looking at: 
When the last business review was (if there was one)
Creating a target every six months after the last business review
We then calculate the business review target PER CUSTOMER and aggregate that up to the CSM. 
This sounded simple at the time, but in practice, turns out to be rather complicated. 
Rather than tweak this existing methodology, I suggest we change the approach entirely. Learning from my mistake, here's how I recommend you approach this in the future. 
At the beginning of the year (and reviewed at the end of each quarter), we generate a business review target proposal, at the customer level.
The data team can help with this using the existing pipeline
Once agreed with the CSM MT, we upload the targets to CRM as a 'Business Review Task' on the customer account. Each required business review will have a task in CRM. 
If we have to change the target, we can change the tasks in CRM
Rather than putting the customer 'On Hold', we can remove tasks or push them into the future. 
We monitor each quarter to ensure that we are adding targets for new live customers
This approach is much better for several reasons: 
Puts ops and management in control
More predictable
Takes pressure off the data team
More flexible when needed
