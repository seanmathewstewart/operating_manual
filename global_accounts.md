Global Accounts

There are two distinct problems: 
1. Mapping out which accounts in the hierarchy make decisions and then aggregating activity to that unit - At the local account, at the parent, or at the global ultimate parent. In other words, where would we create the opportunity for this group of accounts?
2. Treating some accounts as a single unit from a GTM persepctive - Creating a single GTM plan to approach a group of accounts. 

These are distinct problems, and right now we are mixing them up. For example: 
- Thyssenkrupp - We sold into a child account of Thyssenkrupp group. But the very nature of selling into the child account tells us that the opportunity isn't with the global ultimate parent, but rather, with the child accounts. So if this account doesn't make decisions as a global unit, then why are we aggregating all ownership to the DACH squad by default? Therefore we should only treat this account as a group if we have a good reason to from a GTM perspective and we have a GLOBAL account plan to execute upon. Thyssenkrupp has over 100 subsidiraies all over the world, it is unreasonable to assume that we are going to willy-nilly expect the DACH squad to approach all these accounts. There needs to an account plan behind this. 

So when should we assign global ownership to a group of accounts for GTM purposes?
1. We sell into the child or sister account, and there are other interesting accounts in the group. 
2. The decision making unit is at the parent or global ultimate parent level. In other words, the opportunity is with an account further up the hierarchy. => In this case, perhaps we DQ the local account. 
3. The ERP is managed at the parent account, therefore, we know all the subsidaries are on the same ERP. 

So we should not by default treat these as group accounts. This will cause a lot more confusion then it actually solves. Rather, we should proactivly identify the accounts that we want to treat as groups (there are less than 100) and then systematically create account plans in how we apporach these accounts. Namey, we need a process that will: 
1. Identify the intersting groups
2. Identify the interesting child accounts
3. Prioritizes the child accounts
4. Addresses how we sell cross regionally (DACH squad owns accounts in NAMER, etc)
5. Tim approves
6. Flag these accounts in CRM
7. Execute
