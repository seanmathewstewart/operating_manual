
# Segmentation and the Future of ICP Alignment

## TLDR
- We need a better bridge between our target segments and individual accounts
- Industry verticals are not effective at bridging this gap since a vertical does not say enough about where Sana adds value
- Graydon’s Needs Based Horizontals are a step in the right direction, but we can be more product‑focused by using the **Primary Business Cases** Sana solves
- We should align revenue and product teams around the top Primary Business Cases and make that the cornerstone of our GTM engine

## Problem Statement
A GTM team must align around a common ICP. Without this, confusion arises and teams work against each other. While we have a segmentation framework based on channel and product complexity, we struggle to build a GTM motion around these segments.

An ICP definition should tell us:
- What type of companies to target
- Where we are most competitive
- A compelling story of where we add value

The confusion arises in how segmentation translates to accounts.

## From Segmentation to Accounts
Industry verticals (e.g., NAICS) do not explain where Sana adds value. Two accounts in the same segment may share complexity but not business needs.

### Example 1: Supreme Segment
- **Fagron**: sells regulated pharmaceutical components requiring approvals.
- **Palfinger**: sells heavy machinery and manages complex spare parts networks.

Both are complex but in *different ways* — making segment alone insufficient.

### Example 2: Plastics & Rubber Vertical
Customers include:
- Michelin → Tires
- Forbo Flooring → Flooring
- Hydroscan → Hydraulic components

They share a vertical but have very different business cases.

## Aligning GTM Around Primary Business Cases
Instead of verticals, focus on **WHAT** customers produce, **WHO** they sell to, and **HOW** they sell.

Examples:
- Hydroscan and Verder Liquids both produce industrial parts → similar business case.
- Machinery sellers span multiple verticals (Machinery, Transportation, Medical).

Primary Business Cases go deeper than verticals and cut horizontally across them.

This enables us to:
- Identify where we win today and tomorrow
- Tell business‑case‑specific value stories
- Map competitiveness per business case

## Operationalizing Business Cases
Defining the business cases is only step one — embedding them operationally is the challenge.

Each business case needs:
- Customer examples
- Market account identification
- B2B complexity mapping
- Fit scoring
- Product compatibility analysis
- Commissioned research
- Bundled Sana package
- Competitive mapping
- Roadmap alignment
- Opportunity process integration

Every GTM conversation and opportunity should begin with the Primary Business Case.

## Next Steps
If aligned, the next step is formalizing 5–10 Primary Business Cases.

**Potential business cases & examples:**
- Sell Machinery & Heavy Equipment → Palfinger
- Sell Industrial Parts & Consumables → Hydroscan
- Sell Regulated Products → Fagron
- Sell Bulk Goods with Volatile Prices → Orkla
- Sell Customizable Made‑to‑Order Products → Arizona Fireplaces
- Sell Construction‑Related Goods → Forbo
- Sell Medical‑Industry Goods → Aoss, Corza
- Mixed B2B + B2C → *Not a good business case*
- Dealer portals with pricing only → *Not prioritized*
- B2C brands managing B2B → *Not prioritized*
- Highly complex customer agreements → *Too broad*

## FAQs & Other Thoughts
### What about manufacturing?
- ICP revolves around channel & product complexity, not manufacturing vs wholesale.
- Some business cases may apply only to manufacturers.

### What about the pizza segments?
- They may stay, but Primary Business Cases will improve account mapping.

### What about Needs‑Based Horizontals?
- Spirit is good, but still too high‑level and not operationalized.

### Do verticals still matter?
- Yes, when speaking to accounts — but not sufficient for GTM alignment.

### How do we enrich accounts?
- 6‑digit NAICS combinations
- Data vendors
- AI enrichment (prototype exists)

### How to operationalize (WIP)?
- Define Primary (and later Secondary) Business Cases
- Embed into GTM processes
- Account-level estimation
- Opportunity-level primary & secondary validation
- CRM enrichment via NAICS + AI
- Enablement roadmap
- Quarterly reviews & ownership model
