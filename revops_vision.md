
# RevOps Vision

## What are the problems with RevOps?
- We lack a team vision. It feels like we are just a group of individuals working separately. We are not greater than the sum of our parts.
- We are putting out fires at the expense of longer-term and more impactful projects.
- We are not solving problems end to end.
- We never celebrate our successes or reflect on our failures.
- We lack a project roadmap that is aligned to the business priorities.

## What are the problems with the Business?
- Sana struggles to execute our GTM strategy effectively.
- 40% of our event leads have been followed up with.
- MQAs convert to pipeline <5% of the time.
- Win-rate has fallen from 25% to 14%.
- There is no consistent BDR process or results.
- The AEs do not track their activities, and therefore we do not have visibility on their pipeline generation.
- No agreement on which marketing metrics are important to drive alignment.
- Little to no accountability on KPIs.
- There is unclear ownership over processes, applications, and projects across the board. Almost nothing in this company has clear ownership.
- There is a lack of commitment from business stakeholders.
- There is complexity everywhere, leading to misalignment and frustration.

## RevOps Vision
Drive predictable execution.
- Predictability
- Scalability
- Better Decisions

**Enabling predictable growth at scale.**

## Our Operating Principles
- We are consultants, not order takers.
- We believe in end-to-end problem solving, not quick fixes.
- Complexity kills growth. We need to keep things simple.
- We solve problems with data.

## Core Responsibilities
- Clearly articulate our vision for the future of revenue operations.
- Create a roadmap of projects and improvements to realize this vision.
- Proactively communicate and enable the business.
- Manage Standard Operating Procedures to drive predictable results, align teams, and hold people accountable.
- Own data reporting, analysis, and KPI creation.
- Manage the GTM tech stack.

## What does this mean?
For years, the RevOps team has lacked clear vision and purpose. It reacts to business demands rather than proactively guiding the business. It is often one step behind and prioritizes based on the loudest voice, not the biggest impact.

RevOps needs to lead from the front—translating business strategy into predictable processes, owning day-to-day execution, leading meetings, and advising on the best way of doing things.

This transforms RevOps into a strategic partner to the business.

## Four Types of Work

### 1. **Operations**
- Predictable recurring tasks.
- Goal: Schedule, simplify, automate.
- Examples: Monthly Commissions, Quarterly TAL Update, Weekly account checks.

### 2. **Projects**
- One-time sets of work with a clear outcome.
- Goal: Map out, prioritize, schedule — this becomes the roadmap.
- Examples: CX Journey, Subscription Module Changes, CRM Relaunch.

### 3. **Ad-hoc Requests**
- Small tasks, limited scope, can be done by one person.
- Goal: Minimize. Build processes so end users can self-serve.
- Examples: Exporting data, updating a report filter.

### 4. **Fires & Escalations**
- Work due to failures or urgent unplanned issues.
- Goal: Prevention.
- Examples: Email bounce issues, GMV data issues, report refresh failures.

## Three Key Projects

### **1. Define the Operating Rhythm**
**Problem:** No clear operating rhythm; meetings lack data and purpose.

**Objective:** Establish week, month, quarter rhythms for Revenue & Marketing.

**Expected Outcome:** More alignment and predictable execution.

**Key Results:**
- Map out rhythms:
  - Quarterly: Business Review
  - Monthly: Business Check-in
  - Weekly: POD Review, CS Engagement Review
- Define objectives, outcomes, and required data for each meeting.

### **2. CRM Relaunch**
**Problem:** CRM is hostile to users; no ownership; too much is done outside the system.

**Objective:** Clean up, simplify, and enable users.

**Expected Outcome:** Empower end users and reduce RevOps workload.

**Key Results:**
- Map use cases.
- Standardize system views.
- Remove unnecessary fields (“Marie Kondo” the CRM).
- Create enablement plan.
- Define ownership and improvement rhythm.

### **3. Process Mapping / CX Journey**
**Problem:** High complexity, unclear ownership, weak change management.

**Objective:** End-to-end process mapping and simplification across GTM motions and tech stack.

**Key Results:**
- CX Journey
- Events
- HIR
- TAL
- Marketing Campaigns
