2. Advice

It's not secret that Sana has been struggling to grow these last 3 years. When a individual struggles to perform, it's a HR issue. When an entire department struggles, it's a problem with the, market, leadership, product, strategy or with execution. 

To me, it is primarily an execution issue. I am biased towards this because it is the only thing that we have in our control. 

When I think about how we operate as a company, I see a company that lacks focus, not in our market segemnt or in our strategy, but in our execution. We do too much. Last year, we did the plumbing project, the critical mission sprints, and the sales re-org, all at the same time. And because of that lack of focus, we lack speed. So rather than being focused and moving fast, we are complex and slow. This is dangerous place to be in a competitive market.  

So advice to all of you, is to do less. To focus more. To deliberetly choose what we are saying yes to, and what we are saying no to. And lastely always optimize for the simplest and fastest solution possible. 

WIth every new problem you are solving, or you are bringing to the operations team, ask these questions?
1. Does solving this make us a simpler and faster company?
2. Does solving this affect our results?
3. Is this the simplest possible way to move forward?
