V2MoM – Head of Data and AI at Channel Engine

Vision

Data provides a competitive advantage to Channel Engine by driving faster decision-making using analytics and more efficient operations using automations and AI. 

Values

Problem Oriented – Clearly define the problem before defining a solution. 

Simplicity - Always use the simplest possible methods to solve the problem. 

Speed amplifies value - The faster we move, the faster we learn, the more value we can drive. Ship early and ship often. 

Self-service - Enable the company to solve their own problems. 

Scalability - Start with the right foundation, solve problems at the root, ensures that we are not fighting fires in the future. 

Code is King - The more we can develop and maintain our data products in code, the better. Code allows us to version control, gain visibility over operations, and create documentation in a more sustainable way than GUI based applications.

Methods: 

Get close to the business

To drive value, the data team needs to get close to the business. They need to understand both the big picture (vision, goals, strategy) but also the details (processes, systems). The closer they are to the business, the better we can identify and solve problems, the more value they can drive.

This means forming tight relationships with the business stakeholders, enabling our end users, and ensuring they have data in the right place, in the right format at the right time. 

From Dashboards to Data Products

Most data teams are overwhelmed by low value activities such as building dashboards and doing data exports. We need to push dashboarding and exports to business applications as much as possible. And expand the teams horizon to think in terms of products. 

Lay the foundation

Introduce a data lake with medallion architecture. Clean up and eliminate the legacy that weighs down most teams. Push data transformations as far upstream as possible and as far downstream as necessary. Clean data at the source.

Project Management and Team Operating System

To execute effectively, and at speed, we need focus and space

Create the operating rhythm of the team

Introduce a simplified agile system, with a bi-weekly sprint schedule. 

Every project should have: 

Clear objective and expected outcome

Business stakeholder

Main tasks and milestones mapped out and scheduled

We should challenge each project with: 

Are these the complete set of tasks? 

Have we mapped out the hand over moments and dependencies?

How can we complete this project in half the time scheduled? 

Challenge ourselves to ship early and ship often. But also, to keep things as simple as possible.

What about AI?

AI is presented as a technology that is going to change everything. And for some companies, it will completely disrupt their core business (duolingo, chegg) but for most it won’t. So thinking about AI as one thing that needs to be driven from the top, is a mistake.

AI is primarily affecting four areas of the business:

Product Development  - Agentic Commerce

Existing Business Application – AI in Hubspot

New AI First Applications – Gong, Curser

Automations – Open AI API, N8N

The further down this list, the more the data team should be involved and take a leading role.

This means that the main way the data team is going to lead with AI is through automations. AI allows a company to push the boundaries of what can be automated. But what holds back most companies from adopting more automations isn’t technology, it was a: 

Lack of automation strategy

Lack of automation ownership

Lack of clearly defined business processes to automate

So the data team should take the lead in driving automations, AI will be underneath that strategy, but we shouldn’t adopt AI for the sake of adopting AI. We need to go back to our values, namely, starting with the problem and simplicity. Therefore, if we can automate a task without AI, then we shouldn’t use AI. 

My biggest worry is that driving automation at a company requires a level of maturity that most scale ups don’t have. So while we can have a team that can build fast and is capable, if the company isn’t ready to automate, then the value of the data team diminishes.
